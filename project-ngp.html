Nebraska Game and Parks

The Game and Parks work to conserve Nebraska’s natural resources. They were established in 1901 by the Legislature. According to their website, among the list of things they are in charge of is establishing hunting seasons and regulations for game species; managing Nebraska’s state parks, state recreation areas and other public lands; managing the fisheries at numerous public lakes across the state; helping landowners establish good conservation practices on their land; working to conserve Nebraska’s threatened and endangered species; and providing hunter and boater education, as well as other resources for those who wish to learn to enjoy the outdoors. This audience enjoy the outdoors, nature, and everything it has to offer in Nebraska.
